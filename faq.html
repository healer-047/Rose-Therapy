Is therapy confidential?

Yes—confidentiality is central to therapy. We’ll explain how your information is handled and any limits to confidentiality (for example, if there is an immediate risk of serious harm). If you have questions, ask before sharing details.

Who is therapy for?

Therapy can support you if you’re experiencing anxiety, low mood, stress, relationship challenges, or you want a supportive space to understand yourself and build coping tools. It’s also helpful during transitions, grief, or feeling “stuck.”

Are sessions online? What language are they in?

Sessions are held online via video call, in English.

Do you work with clients outside the UK?

Yes—Rose Therapy works with clients in the UK and worldwide. Booking uses UK time (Europe/London), and we show your local time on the booking page.

How do cancellations and rescheduling work?

Please see the cancellation policy in our Terms. If you need to reschedule, message as soon as possible so we can try to help.

How does payment work?

Payment is arranged after we confirm your appointment time. We’ll share instructions via WhatsApp or email.

How do timezones work?

Time slots are listed in UK time (Europe/London). On the booking page, your selection is shown in both UK time and your local timezone. If you’re unsure, message us and we’ll confirm your local time.

Is Rose Therapy suitable for emergencies?

No. If you are in immediate danger or at risk of harm, call your local emergency number. In the UK: 999 (emergency) or 111 (non‑emergency). You can also contact Samaritans (UK & ROI) on 116 123.
